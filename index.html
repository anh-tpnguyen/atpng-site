Background

I grew up between cultures, which ignited my passion for understanding how development unfolds across diverse contexts. My background informs my commitment to culturally sensitive research and practice, particularly with Vietnamese-American and immigrant communities. I am bilingual in English and Vietnamese, and I strive to make psychology accessible and meaningful for populations who are often underserved in research and clinical settings.

Scholarly Work
Research & Publications
Research Focus

My research examines how early adverse childhood experiences shape developmental trajectories in diverse cultural contexts. I am particularly interested in the intersection of trauma, acculturation stress, and resilience among children of immigrant families. I employ mixed-methods approaches, combining quantitative longitudinal methods with community-based qualitative inquiry.
